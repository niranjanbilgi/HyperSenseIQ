What does this mean for your well?
Decline projection
The forward production curve uses PHF to anchor the starting rate. A well that outperformed the model projects a higher future production baseline — and therefore more EOR upside.
EOR incremental recovery
A well performing above its model produces more incremental barrels from EOR. PHF scales the ΔN_eff calculation — so outperforming wells rank higher in the FFUS priority list.
Project economics (NPV)
The fiscal calculation scales projected revenue by PHF. A well lagging its model (PHF < 0.90) may show negative NPV at current oil prices — a flag to investigate before committing EOR CAPEX.
Executive portfolio view
The PHF column in the Executive View instantly distinguishes wells that are delivery-ready from those requiring investigation. Green = deploy. Amber = monitor. Red = investigate before spending.
EUR (recoverable reserves)
PHF-adjusted EUR = Arps base EUR × PHF. This is shown alongside the model EUR in the Decline tab — giving you both the theoretical reserve and the history-anchored estimate.
EOR deployment timing
Wells with PHF < 0.90 flag a wellbore investigation recommendation before EOR commitment. Poor recent performance may indicate skin damage or fluid composition changes that EOR alone cannot fix.

TZ_pen interpretation:
<0.20 → low risk · conformance adequate
0.20–0.50 → moderate · conformance control advised
>0.50 → severe · injection EOR methods penalised
P_EOR_adj — Adjusted EOR scores vs raw P_EOR
Formula: P_EOR_adj = P_EOR_raw × W_mod × (1 − TZ_pen × 0.15)
