EOR Screening · Eight Methods, One Answer
Eight methods.
One answer.
HyperSenseIQ evaluates all eight EOR methods simultaneously across six physics domains — then ranks them by P_EOR score for your specific reservoir.
The P_EOR score is adjusted by three reality checks: W_mod (wettability — is the rock oil-wet or water-wet?), CF_mod (formation chemistry — will the injected chemicals survive?), and TZ_pen (thief zone penalty — is there a high-permeability streak stealing your flood?). Each modifier is now visible as a live diagnostic card in the EOR Screening tab. No single score fits all wells. HyperSenseIQ shows you exactly why.
Chemistry · Will the Chemicals Survive?
Will the chemicals
survive the formation?
Before spending crores on polymer or ASP flooding, HyperSenseIQ checks whether the formation water chemistry will allow those chemicals to work — or destroy them before they reach the oil. Six diagnostic indices, computed automatically.
For Ankleshwar Hazad Member — SSI 2.2 (severe CaCO₃ scaling) means scale inhibitor is mandatory before any chemical EOR. UST has CF_mod = 1.00 always — no chemicals, no chemistry penalty. Adverse formation conditions actually widen UST's advantage.
Now the engineer does not just read a static index. Enter real formation water ions into the Live Water Chemistry Calculator — Ca²⁺, HCO₃⁻, SO₄²⁻, TDS, pH, temperature. SSI (Stiff-Davis 1952), WFAR and scale inhibitor dose compute instantly. The fluid compatibility matrix updates live. Dead numbers become a living diagnostic.
